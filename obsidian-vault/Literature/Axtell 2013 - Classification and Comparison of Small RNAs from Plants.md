## One-Sentence Academic Gist

Source-reported content: Axtell proposes a hierarchical classification of endogenous plant DCL/AGO small RNAs, placing a primary distinction between hairpin-derived small RNAs and dsRNA-derived siRNAs, then comparing their biogenesis, mechanisms, conservation, and unresolved questions.

## Research Question and Scope

Source-reported content: The review asks how endogenous plant small RNAs can be classified in a biologically useful way and how the major classes compare in biogenesis, mechanism, function, and evolutionary conservation. The author explicitly focuses on endogenous DCL/AGO small RNAs in plants and largely excludes viral/pathogen-associated small RNAs, synthetic small RNAs, and deeper discussion of non-core biogenesis factors.

Reasonable inference: The article is useful as a conceptual taxonomy for organizing plant small RNA literature, especially when comparing miRNA, heterochromatic siRNA, secondary siRNA, and NAT-siRNA studies.

## Limitations and Cautions

> [!warning] Evidence Boundary
> This review summarizes many primary studies. Do not convert its claims into primary experimental evidence in downstream evidence matrices.

- Source-reported content: the author notes that a classification system is an intellectual construct and may change as knowledge accumulates.
- Source-reported content: Arabidopsis has shaped much plant small RNA thinking, but the author cautions that Arabidopsis is atypical because of its small genome, few functional transposable elements, and low heterozygosity.
