# Phased Secondary siRNA

Secondary siRNAs are described in [[Axtell 2013 - Classification and Comparison of Small RNAs from Plants]] as siRNAs whose dsRNA precursor production is stimulated by an upstream small RNA. Phased secondary siRNAs are generated from successive DCL processing from a consistent dsRNA terminus.

## Key Points

- Many known secondary siRNAs require RDR6 and DCL4.
- Phasing is a useful computational signature, but not all secondary siRNAs must be phased.
- Some secondary siRNAs act in trans and can coordinate repression across related gene families.

> [!warning]
> Treat "trans-acting" and "phased" as overlapping but not identical labels.
