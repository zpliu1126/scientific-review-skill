# Life Science Review Writing Reference

Use this reference for general life science review planning and synthesis across plant, animal, human, microbial, molecular, cellular, genomic, and biomedical topics.

## Review Scope

- Define the review question before drafting.
- State inclusion and exclusion boundaries, such as organism, disease, tissue, method, time period, evidence type, or application.
- Distinguish review types when relevant: narrative review, mini-review, systematic review, scoping review, evidence map, or perspective.
- State whether the output is based on full text, abstracts, metadata, user notes, or searched sources.

## Evidence Organization

- Organize by scientific claims, mechanisms, evidence levels, models, methods, or controversies.
- Avoid paper-by-paper lists unless the user explicitly asks for an annotated bibliography.
- Separate primary studies, reviews, preprints, datasets, methods papers, and clinical guidelines.
- Track negative results, conflicting findings, and studies that fail to replicate.

## Synthesis

- Compare convergence and disagreement across studies.
- Explain whether disagreement may come from model system, tissue, developmental stage, treatment, population, assay, statistics, or batch effects.
- Separate correlation, perturbation, mechanism, and validation.
- Do not turn single-study findings into field-wide consensus.

## Figures and Tables

- Use evidence matrices for multi-paper comparison.
- Use mechanism diagrams only when mechanisms are source-supported or clearly labeled as model synthesis.
- Use gap tables to separate known evidence, uncertainty, and missing experiments.

## Pre-Output Evidence Check

Before finalizing a review answer, check:

- Are all factual claims tied to a provided or verified source?
- Are unsupported claims marked `[source needed]`?
- Are available but unchecked claims marked `[needs verification]`?
- Are source-reported content, reasonable inference, and model synthesis separated?
- Is any causal wording stronger than the evidence allows?
- Is the basis of the answer stated: full text, abstract, metadata, user notes, or searched sources?
