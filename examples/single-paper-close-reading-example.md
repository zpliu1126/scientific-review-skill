# Example: Single-Paper Close Reading

## Source Basis

- Basis: abstract only
- Full citation or identifier: User-provided abstract for Plant Transcriptome Paper 1
- Missing metadata: authors, title, journal, year, DOI, PMID

## Research Question

The paper asks how drought stress alters leaf transcriptomes in a crop species and which candidate genes may be associated with drought-response pathways.

## Key Findings

| Finding | Source anchor | Evidence type | Validation status | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Drought treatment changed expression of stress-responsive genes | Abstract sentence 2 | RNA-seq/DEG | Not reported in provided text | Supports expression response, not gene function |
| GO/KEGG terms related to ABA signaling and osmotic stress were enriched | Abstract sentence 3 | Enrichment analysis | Not reported in provided text | Suggests pathway association, not mechanism proof |
| Selected DEGs were tested by qRT-PCR/qPCR | Abstract sentence 4 | Expression validation | qRT-PCR/qPCR for selected genes | Validates expression trends only |

## Source-Reported Content

The authors report drought-responsive DEGs, enriched stress-related pathways, and qRT-PCR/qPCR validation for selected genes in the studied crop tissue.

## Reasonable Inference

The results are consistent with transcriptional remodeling under drought stress, but the abstract alone does not establish that the candidate genes control drought tolerance.

## Model Synthesis

This paper could support a review section on transcriptome-based candidate gene discovery in crop drought responses, especially if paired with studies that include mutant, overexpression, VIGS, CRISPR, or physiological validation.

## Claims Requiring More Support

- The candidate genes directly improve drought tolerance. [source needed]
